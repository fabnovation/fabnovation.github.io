    Kaoss Pad Rehousing

    Learning Outcomes:

    • Explore possibilities in rehousing a touch pad onto guitar
    • Identify ways to create non-disruptively intervention
    • Fabricate and install prototype
    • Test and perform
      •

    OBJECTIVES

    The Kaoss Pad KP3 by Korg is a real-time audio effects processor commonly used by DJs to manipulate sound on the fly. Made famous by several artists over the past decade or so, KP3 caught the attention of guitarists over the world ever since the album debute of the infamous rock band MUSE called The Resistance. With the touch of his finger, Matt Bellamy (lead singer and guitarist of MUSE) was able to transform his Manson guitar into a brillant atmosphere of high/ low pitched sound with ethereal ambience. Captured by the flexibility to manipulate sound in realtime, the Kaoss Pad Rehousing project attempts to look at ways to attach a touch pad to a guitar and a KP3+. This project will be further developed and serves as a starting point for a series of re-designing and enhancements.


    DESIGN INTENT

    The manson guitar is an electric guitar embedded with a touchpad within the guitar body. There are commercial services such as AmpTone Lab or Crimsonguitars that cater retrofitting services of touch pad, along with all the electronics and switches into any guitars. However, this involves removing a good portion of the guitar body by drilling or using a CNC router. This method is disruptive and consequential to altering the original tone of the guitar. The design intend for the Kaoss Pad Rehousing project is to ensure that all interventions occur outside the guitar body and rehousing the touch pad will be kept at a minimum cost.

    DESIGN STRATEGY

    The Kaoss Pad rehousing consists of 3 areas of intervention:

    • Extraction of touch pad from the KP3+ machine and
    • Re-wire the snipped points to a connector.
    • Rehouse the extracted touch pad using 3D printing.

    DESIGN DEVELOPMENT

    Extraction

    This process involves dismantling the entire KP3+ machine and ensuring that the components remain intact after extraction of the touch screen. There are 3 circuit boards in total and a plastic LED lights underlay to remove before the touch screen can be safely taken out to modify. Below are some pictorial guide in performing the extraction. Ensure that all screws and knobs from the bottom and the sides of device are removed before attempting to de-mount the cover.

    Re-wiring Connections

    Attempt 01 (incomplete)
    The re-wiring process involves snipping the touch screen ribbon wire and solder the ends to USB connectors. Before soldering the ribbon wires, the copper connections needs to be exposed by scraping/ peeling off the top plastic protector as shown in the extreme left image below. Ensure that the connections do not cross each other to avoid shorting the circuit. It is important to note that there are commercial connectors available for purchase and it can be done without having the need to solder. Soldering for this project was adopted solely for experimentation purposes and keeping budget low. Once connections are completed and checked, re-assemble KP3+.

    During the process, the touch screen was accidentally broken and a new touch screen had to be purchased. The process had to be back-tracked after arrival of shipment.

    Rehousing Touch Pad

    3D drawing using Rhino
    Now that the new screen has arrived, 3D drawing can begin. The measurements were taken directly from a Gibson Studio electric guitar and a downloaded 3D guitar model for simulation. In addition, some dimension references were also taken from a Fishmen Triple Play mounting support.

    Drawn using Rhino (3D software), all profiles were created 2 dimensionally and then extruded. The slightly more challenging component to create was the USB connector mount where the edges were rounded. This can be done using the command "Fillet Edge" under the "Solid" menu tab.     Installation

    Upon completion of printing components, the parts were glued together and the touch screen together with the modified ribbon wire encased. The ribbon wire had to be bended and taped so that it can be fitted within the casing. In addition to the touch screen, the tinted laminated plastic sheet from KP3+ was also readapted to create a dark screen. Lastly, the USB connector was fitted with the 3D printed mount and glued in place. Similarly, with the USB connector added to KP3+, it was attached by means of double-sided tape.

    REFLECTIONS

    It's quite an amazing feeling to be able to put together an idea of rehousing a product, customizing it to a specific need and practice. For now, the critical question would be how to further improve the modification and perhaps to avoid some of the errors encountered during the process. Here are some pointers for reflection:

    1. Re-housing Design:
      • Consider wireless connections like bluetooth instead of USB connectors. Besides having the advantage of wireless connection, this method avoids loose connections between the device and the USB cable.
      • Should consider mounting device nearer to guitar strings to allow easy accessibility.
      • Consider metal angle plates for mounting 3D parts to guitar. ABS plastic may be strong enough but once the screw tightens, the 3D ABS plastic printed angle mount got disfigured to misalignment of about 5%. For this project, the quick remedy was to use rubber supports to neutralize the difference.

    2. Re-wiring:
      • Consider purchasing connectors for touch screen installation instead of soldering as it takes up time to solder accurately and this method is prone to shorting and might create unneccessary grounding issues if the wiring is not taken care of properly.
      • Consider rubber sleeves at all soldered connections

    Overall, it's been an interesting process. Looking forward to further on the design!
