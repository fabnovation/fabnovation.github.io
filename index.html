    About FN

    Fabrication . Innovation

    Keith Tan

    Born in Singapore, Keith graduated from RMIT University, Melbourne, Australia (1999-2003). During his studies as well as being an architect in the industry, he has won international architectural competitions and received recognitions from the University and company that he represented. Fueled by passion and desire for innovation, Keith embarked on the journey as an educator at Singapore Polytechnic, School of Architecture and the Built Environment (ABE), on November 2008. Besides being a lecturer, Keith is spearheading the Diploma in Architecture (DARCH) Fablab, specialising in Parametric Designs and Rapid Prototyping. Currently, Keith is also involved in supporting ABE as a multi-media designer and webmaster.

    Fabnovation

    Fabnovation was a title coined by Keith in January 2016 for a website designed to showcase completed assignments for the Fab Academy Diploma. Developed to empower skills for fablab users, the diploma teaches principles and applications of digital fabrication through a global network of more than 500 fablabs. Inspired by the mode of delivery and the concept of open source environment, Fabnovation seeks to expand and to cultivate innovation through ideation and to spur on the culture of making globally. With strong ties at SP Fablab and DARCH Fablab, Fabnovation looks forward in creating a resource efficient environment through motivating individuals to share, innovate, fabricate, network and ultimately to transform the fabric our cities.

    DARCH FabLab

    The Singapore Polytechnic School of Architecture and the Built Environment (ABE) has the first and most established Diploma in Architecture (DARCH) course in Singapore. Being an advancement in applications of technology, DARCH Fablab seeks to inspire and aims to bridge the gaps between design processes, technologies and manufacturing in Architecture and Architectural Education.
